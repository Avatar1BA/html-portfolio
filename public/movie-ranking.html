The Best Movies According to Saranath

My top Three Movies of all-time.

Three Idiots (2009)

Three Idiots (2009), directed by Rajkumar Hirani, is a heartwarming comedy-drama about friendship, education, and following one’s passion. The film stars Aamir Khan, R. Madhavan, Sharman Joshi, Kareena Kapoor, and Boman Irani. It tells the story of three engineering students—Rancho, Farhan, and Raju—who navigate the pressures of India’s rigid education system. Rancho, a free-spirited genius, inspires his friends to think differently, chase their dreams, and value knowledge over grades. Through humor, emotion, and social critique, the film emphasizes creativity, curiosity, and living life on one’s own terms, leaving audiences with the mantra: “All is well.”

Fresh perspective on education: The film challenges the rigid, exam-driven system by promoting curiosity, creativity, and passion. It’s not just entertaining—it sparks reflection on how we learn and why grades shouldn’t define intelligence.

Emotional depth with humor: Balancing comedy and heartfelt drama, Three Idiots makes you laugh while tugging at your heartstrings. The friendship between Rancho, Farhan, and Raju is relatable and inspiring, reminding us of the importance of support and loyalty.

Universal life lessons: With its iconic mantra “All is well”, the movie leaves viewers with hope and courage to pursue their dreams. It’s a timeless story that resonates across generations, making it both enjoyable and meaningful.

Baghban (2003)

Baghban (2003), directed by Ravi Chopra, is a poignant family drama starring Amitabh Bachchan, Hema Malini, Salman Khan, and Mahima Chaudhry. The film tells the story of Raj and Pooja Malhotra, a couple married for over 40 years, who sacrifice everything to raise their four sons. However, when Raj retires, the children refuse to care for their parents together, forcing them to live apart. Their pain highlights themes of love, sacrifice, and neglect in modern families. Amidst heartbreak, their adopted son Alok (Salman Khan) stands by them, proving that true bonds are built on respect and affection.

Rang De Basanti (2006)

Rang De Basanti (2006), directed by Rakeysh Omprakash Mehra, is a powerful drama blending youth rebellion with historical patriotism. The film stars Aamir Khan, Siddharth, Sharman Joshi, Kunal Kapoor, Atul Kulkarni, Soha Ali Khan, and Alice Patten. It follows a group of carefree college friends who, while helping a British filmmaker document India’s freedom fighters, awaken to the realities of corruption and injustice in modern society. Inspired by the sacrifices of revolutionaries like Bhagat Singh, they transform from indifferent youth into passionate activists. The film delivers a stirring message about responsibility, courage, and the power of young voices to spark change.
